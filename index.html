Brain Tumor Segmentation and 3D Reconstruction from MRI data

This project seeks to expand and improve on existing methods for brain tumor segmentation from MRI scans. Manual tumor segmentation from trained medical professionals is costly and prone to human error or inconsistency. We are training a UNet-based segmentation model and 3D reconstruction and building visualization tool with the goal to automate the tedious task of segmentation and also help doctors better visualize patient tumors and make more informed decisions for treatment.

Active Aerodynamics of Morphing Camber

Changing the amount of camber, or curvature, present in an airfoil is a powerful means of controlling the forces and moments that it generates under fluid flow. Furthermore, the control authority provided is significant even if only a modest portion of the airfoil chord has the ability to change camber. Under this project, a study was made with the application for a Horizontal Axis Wind Turbine (HAWT) to use the ability to dynamically change the blade camber with programmed servos to give max aerodynamic efficiency in different wind speeds.

Affiliation: National Institute of Engineering
Course: Final Year Project/Thesis (Major Project) @ National Institute of Engineering
Status: Complete

Low Speed Open Circuit Wind Tunnel

An open circuit low-speed wind tunnel was designed and fabricated to simulate fluid flow for flow analysis for aerodynamic subjects. The test section measures 57.5 cm in length with the whole tunnel being 2.7 meters long. Currently, the wind tunnel is being displayed in the Heat Transfer laboratory at NIE, Mysuru, and is operational and intended to be used for further studies.

Affiliation: National Institute of Engineering
Course: Final Year Project/Thesis (Major Project) @ National Institute of Engineering
Status: Complete
